# ZUP LΛBS Basic Style Guide

## Brand Tone

Credible, sharp, modern, minimal.

The voice should feel operational rather than corporate-fluffy:

- clear over clever
- precise over abstract
- grounded in execution, compliance, accountability and field reality

## Typography

- Display and headings: `Space Grotesk`
- Body copy and UI text: `Manrope`

Usage guidance:

- Use `Space Grotesk` for hero lines, section headings and strong product titles.
- Use `Manrope` for navigation, body copy, metadata, buttons and support text.
- Keep tracking slightly tighter on big headlines and more open on small uppercase labels.

## Colour System

- Background: near-black charcoal
- Surface panels: layered dark greys with low-contrast borders
- Primary text: soft white
- Secondary text: muted grey
- Accent approach: mostly monochrome, with product logos carrying their own colour where needed

Core page palette:

- `#050505`
- `#0f0f0f`
- `#141414`
- `#f2f2f2`
- `#adadad`

## Layout

- Use wide negative space and strong panel framing.
- Prefer rounded containers with subtle borders rather than heavy decoration.
- Motion should be restrained and purposeful.
- Desktop layouts can feel architectural, but mobile should stack cleanly and read quickly.

## Logo Usage

- Use the white ZUP LΛBS logo on dark backgrounds for primary brand moments.
- Use the cleaner product marks wherever possible instead of screenshots.
- Keep generous clear space around all logos.
- Avoid stretching logos or placing them into visually busy regions.

## Lambda Usage

The lambda character `Λ` is part of the ZUP LΛBS and SHERPΛ brand language.

Recommended use:

- brand marks and brand names: `ZUP LΛBS`, `SHERPΛ`
- prominent display words where it feels intentional: `Λustralian`, `Λustralia`

Avoid overusing lambda substitutions inside dense body copy. It works best in high-visibility brand moments.
